    Take one on Kickstarter

    Dec 12, 2019

    In 2nd year my roommate and I used to brain storm business ideas and problems we could build solutions for on the daily. Although there were many problems we had no idea on how to solve, a few fell through the cracks that were doable. One such idea was the Edison box. This was just a monthly circuit building kit. We created our first monthly kit, corresponding website, and made a video at home, on a home budget. And Launched on kickstarter. This was going to get funded in a week and get us the initial capital we needed to bulk order parts from china! Right? No, helll no, we were rookies who knew nothing about getting our project funded. We kept the project up for about 14 days before canceling it and got about $250 by getting 10 users to signup during the first two days. Once the project was buried as new projects came up, no one saw it and it didn’t get funding. It was a good idea but our execution was terrible. Who knew marketing was very important. Since then I’ve gone on to learn about and have launched successful ads on fb, reddit, google and all sorts of social media platforms with a high conversion rate. Had I known this prior to our kickstarter we would’ve done quite well. This was one of many side hustles I worked on during my university days. This was take one on kickstarter, I’m certain I will have projects in the future that I could potentially launch and get funded through kickstarter.


    On setting and achieving goals

    Dec 1, 2019

    Sometime after grade 3, I realized there was more to school than just going to classes, I started learned about other opportunities in school. I loved track and field day. I became more aware of it in grade 4 and enjoyed the day, won a few ribbons and decided I wanted to win more ribbons next year and place higher. I also wanted to win so I could go on this day trip to a different school and compete with others, get free lunch and enjoy the day off from actual school. So when spring rolled around in 2008 I was in grade 5. I started practicing daily, I used to run, I used to practice running long jumps, standing long jumps. I was competing with my friends and as a result I believe we both got better. We had his older sister tracking our results on a clipboard like the actual thing, I even went as far as to getting the grid paper form school instead of making grid lines with a ruler. Well, track and field day rolled around in grade 5. And I had great results. I placed top 3 in five out of the six events, I got my first ever 1st place ribbon in triple jump, I placed 2nd in running long jump, 2nd in standing long jump and 2nd in the 400 meters race losing to the same two guy for every 2nd place ribbon I had(their genes were definitely athletes’ genes, but I did beat them in the triple jump). Lastly I placed 3rd in 100m dash. During all those years I was never able to place in shot-put. I don’t think I got the technique right nor did I have the build to push a heavy ball away from my body as far as possible. I went on to represent my school at the next level of the competition and got that school day trip. The following year same situation, I practiced and got ready for the events and represented my school at the next level. When I say practice, these weren’t just whatever drills, they were timed drills, where I used to track my result and compare my progress. Anyways I write this is retrospect of how when my vision was much more narrow, I used to set a goal, make a plan and execute on it, I was actually able to get the results. I write this to remind myself that when there is a plan and you put in the time to execute, attaining your goals becomes a very doable thing. Over the next couple of years, I would go on to set targets to get certain awards and prizes and actually did end up getting them, until one day I lost taste for awards and prizes… story for another time.

    The story about my first win

    The first contest I ever won was an art contest. I was in Grade 1, had just moved back to India after spending about 9 months in Canada. (My parents would later decide Canada is the place to be in order to provide better opportunities for my brother and I. We would end up shifting back to Toronto in a year’s time). I remember the night before the contest I had tactfully decided what I was going to draw, the theme was supposed to be around India’s Independence or Republic day, I can’t remember which but we were supposed to draw a flag. So the night before I quickly sketched how I would draw the flag and decided to make it look 3D and draw the flag as if wind was blowing through it and focus on small details like lettings the strings fly in all directions (result of the wind), add steps to the flag mount, draw additional imagery in the background to give it a realistic feel. At that point I had no intention in winning the contest, I was just trying to make the best drawing I possibly could. The next day at school we had an hour to complete our drawing and submit it. I did it, enjoyed the hour, and forgot about it. At the end of the day as I am heading back to my ride home a lady who worked for the school tracked me down told me I had won the art contest and gave me a small gift. I took it home, opened it and found a 24 set of oil pastels. I treasured those pastel crayons and used them carefully.


    The "school busses" in India

    When I was a very small boy just starting school in India. I used to get rides to and from school like almost every other kid. These school busses weren't actual school busses kids so luxuriously enjoy in North America. No, the school bus options were either a rickshaw(made for 3 adults max) or Small white van(made for 6 adults), protrayed in hollywood as the stranger van that abducts you. Each of these vehicles squeezed as many kids in as possible, what safety concerns? So this is what I remember; I was this 4 or 5 year old kid in juinor kindergarten. I would wake up get ready for school and mentally prepare myself for the terrible shitshow of a ride to school. I was the 5th or 6th kid to get picked up so initially there would be some room to sit. But as more kids were picked up I got pushed to the rightmost side. There were about 10-12 kids picked up everyday in that rickshaw. It was so uncomfortable and that is an understatement. To make matters worse, my head would be right up againts the metal frame on the right side. Each bump or pothole on the road(and there were countless) banged my head up againt that frame. Man was that a struggle.

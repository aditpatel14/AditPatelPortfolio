    Early Schooling

    I did most of my schooling in Toronto. For grades 1-12 I went to decent public schools with great extracurricular programs and dedicated teachers. Through the various robotics programs I was involved in I knew early on(when an engineer came in for a talk in grade 8 and mentioned you get a ring) that I wanted to go the engineering route.

    University

    Building things was my calling. I enrolled into Engineering at McMaster University in Hamilton, Ontario after graduating from hichschool. At the end of first year I had to decided a field to special in. I wanted the ability to build anything from scratch, bottom up and so i needed to enroll in a program that would let me touch all aspects of engineering a product, in the end it came down to 2 options, mechatronics engineering or Software Engineer embedded systems design. I looked into the specific courses and the latter aligned better with what I wanted to learn. In June 2019 I graduated with a B.Eng from McMaster and can call myself an engineer.

    On goinging education

    You dont stop learning when you leave school. I've completed more courses online than I can recall. Would have to trace different accounts on different websites like Udemy, Udacity, Stackskills, edX, youtube to figure out the whole list. These courses range in all fields. When I get really interested in a topic and my curisity has been triggered I enjoy putting everything aside and going down the rabbit hole until I am satisfied with the answers I find. Oh and howstuffworks.com is one of my favourite websites.

    Reflection on the education systems

    Currently its a one size fits all scenario and this does not work. I began getting frustrated with how school works towards the end of highschool when I was forced to take courses I didn't want to over courses I had an interest in. The semantics of how students are graded and the large variance between schools also leads to frustrations. How is it possible that kids are expected to learn without first being inspired to learn, a 15 year old kid who has no interest in certain things will not learn through osmosis of being at school and this unfortunately is the basis of how school works. Who thought that just telling kids to go to school will make them want to learn things. There are changes that need to be made at a high level of how educational instuitions work and also at the lower level of the semantics and metrics used. This is just at the conceptual level.

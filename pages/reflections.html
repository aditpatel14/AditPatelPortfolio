    To those who discourage "jack of all trades"

    I don't buy the BS that people cannot master more than one specific field, ie, jack of all trades master of none. If someone puts in the time to master all trades then they will be masters of those trades. Even though time to learn different diciplines is zero-summed. Cross field exposure will allow the individual to have insights that specialized individuals are not capable of, making jack of all trades a non zero-sum.

    The coming wave of comsumer tech

    I envy the students of tomorrow. Within the next 10 years much like how computers changed school, a new gadget will do the same thing. Holographic Augmeted Reality will simply make learning so much more interactive and intuitive. Imagine not having to visualize functions or graphing them on paper like what I had to do, imagine being able to see them in 3D. Imagine going to bio class but instead of just pictures in a text book you could take apart the innerworkings of a cell right in your hands.

    On how to learn something

    I dont know where I came across this, but years ago I heard the best way to understand something is to watch it over and over again until the magic goes away, and this was refering to becoming a great actor. I like to do the same thing with engineered products, I like striping away a complex mechanism bit by bit until I finally understand all the underlying principles and it just makes logical sense. This happened to me with the computer. I was so amazed at how Steve Wozinack made the Apple 1. Then I looked into how it could be done, and kept inching away until all that was left were logical step. Now the invention although amazing doesent seem like a magical illusion, I can trace how he came up with the idea and we go to where we are today. Its amazing. I intend to apply this to all things that intrigue me throughout life.

Android app: quiq

A significant problem university students encounter is the fear of asking questions in lecture amidst a large crowd. So we dec,ide,d to make an app that lets student ask questions anonymously.

Our app allows student to ask teachers question in real-time. Once a student posts a question anonymously other students can see that question and up-vote it if they are wondering the same thing. After a certain amount of up-votes are reached the teacher will see the question on their laptop and be able to answer. Our software also saves the voice recording of the answer so that the answer can be reached later on.

  • Where: Built within 24hours at DeltaHacks
  • When: January 2016
  • Link: Check out the repo

android app: ezpz

Members form our FIRST Robotics Teams 188 and Team 610 would often go on food runs when we are at school working late in the evening. The struggle to remember everyone's order and keep track of who owes who how much money is very real.

When you are going on a food run you open EZPZ and notify your friends. Your friends send you requests for what they want. You than take this list to the store and order everything. The APP stores the data of who ordered what on CLOUDANT. Monetary transactions can also be done by taking the picture of the credit card of the person who owes you money. This transaction works and is very secure.

This project was a UofTHacks Hackathon project. Our team placed as top 5 for Software Hacks and were finalists out of the other 500 people who were participating at the hackathon

  • Where: UfTHacks
  • When: Feb 2015
  • Link: Check out this project!

Firebot

Built a robust robot from scratch and programmed it to maneuver through a maze on it's own to find a fire and blow it out. An Arduino micro-controller was used to control the different motors, light sensors, infrared sensors and ultrasonic sensors.

  • Date: June 2015

Team 188 Woburn Robotics

During my four years at Woburn C.I I was an active member of Woburn's FIRST Robotics Team 188. Annually we would create robots from scrtach to accomplish the specific mission for that year's competition. In my final two year I had the chance to lead the electrical team as the Electrical Head and got to work with incoming students and pass down the experience and knowledge I had gained throughout my time on the team.

The robot you see about was ranked 19th in the world and competed at the Championship event in St. Louis where we reached semi-finals on our division! It also had the highest autonomous score in the world for the championship event.
